# GTM.md -> Go-To-Market Strategy

## Who this is actually for

The main user is probably a small startup team that adopted AI tools very quickly
without ever properly reviewing the spend afterwards.

Usually this looks like:
- engineers using Cursor or Claude because they prefer it
- product people using ChatGPT for brainstorming and docs
- a founder approving subscriptions one-by-one over Slack
- multiple overlapping subscriptions across a 5–20 person team

Most of these companies are not intentionally overspending. They just never
stopped to audit what everyone is paying for because the individual costs feel
small compared to payroll or cloud infrastructure.

The people most likely to care about this are:
- CTOs
- engineering managers
- founding engineers
- small startup founders

Especially teams where AI tools became part of the workflow very quickly over
the last 1–2 years.

Secondary users are solo founders or indie hackers paying personally for
multiple AI subscriptions without really tracking usage.

---

## How users would probably discover this

I don’t think this is a product people actively search for yet because most
teams still treat AI subscriptions as small operational expenses.

The trigger is usually one of these moments:
- noticing the monthly AI spend suddenly becoming much larger
- realizing multiple people are paying for similar tools
- preparing for budgeting or cost-cutting
- scaling from a 3-person team to a 10–20 person team
- seeing discussions online about “AI tool sprawl”

Places where I think users would realistically come from:
- Hacker News
- Reddit startup/dev communities
- founder Twitter/X
- Indie Hackers
- Product Hunt
- word-of-mouth sharing of audit results

---

## Early distribution strategy

The easiest way to get initial users would probably be founder-led outreach.

That means:
- talking directly with startup founders and engineers
- posting audit screenshots and findings online
- sharing real examples of overlapping subscriptions
- using the free audit itself as the acquisition funnel

I think the most effective content would be practical rather than promotional.

For example:
- “We audited our AI spend and found 3 inactive subscriptions”
- “Here’s how a 10-person team ended up paying for 4 overlapping AI tools”
- “AI subscriptions quietly became one of our biggest software expenses”

Those kinds of posts feel more believable than generic “try my startup”
promotion.

---

## Why this could spread organically

The shareable audit result is probably the strongest growth loop in the product.

If a founder runs an audit and discovers meaningful overlap or savings, there’s
a decent chance they share the result internally or publicly.

That creates natural curiosity because most startup teams are currently
experimenting with multiple AI tools and don’t really have visibility into the
overall spend.

The product works better as a utility/tool than as a heavily branded SaaS
product early on.
