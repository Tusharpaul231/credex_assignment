## Interview 1 - Arup Paul
**Role:** ML Engineer & Project Lead  
**Company:** Quantiphi  
**Date:** 2026-05-10

### Summary
Arup currently uses both Claude Pro and ChatGPT Plus and spends around $40/month on AI tools. His company reimburses AI subscriptions because they are considered essential engineering infrastructure. He explained that most ML/SDE engineers on his team actively rely on AI tools daily, while other departments use them less heavily.

One of the most important insights from this interview was that teams intentionally keep overlapping tools because each tool has different strengths. Claude is preferred for complex coding and architecture work, while ChatGPT is preferred for multimodal tasks, research, and brainstorming. This validated the idea that overlap alone does not necessarily mean waste.

Another strong insight was around “Shadow AI.” Arup described how engineers often purchase their own subscriptions independently, making it difficult for companies to track utilization and actual ROI. He mentioned that managers currently lack visibility into whether subscriptions are heavily used or simply forgotten “zombie” subscriptions.

### Key quotes
> “I don't see it as a cost; I see it as ROI.”

> “Most companies are dealing with Shadow AI.”

> “It’s hard to tell if a seat is being used 8 hours a day or if it’s just a zombie subscription someone forgot to cancel.”

### What changed after this interview
- Added stronger emphasis on overlapping subscriptions and utilization tracking
- Positioned AIAudit more as a visibility tool than just a cost-cutting tool
- Improved messaging around “inactive spend” and “tool sprawl”
- Validated that engineering teams often need multiple AI tools simultaneously

## Interview 2 - Sanchal Ghosh
**Role:** SDE II  
**Company:** Makerable  
**Date:** 2026-05-11

### Summary
Sanchal currently uses only ChatGPT Go and spends around ₹400/month personally. Unlike larger engineering teams with multiple subscriptions, his setup is lightweight and individually managed. However, he still expressed concern about AI tools becoming expensive over time, especially if multiple subscriptions start stacking together.

He mentioned that pricing and usage tracking could become difficult once someone starts using several tools simultaneously. He also confirmed that he compares pricing before subscribing and would find an automated AI spend analysis tool useful.

Although his spending level was relatively low compared to other interviewees, this conversation helped validate the secondary audience: solo developers and small teams who want clarity before their AI stack becomes expensive.

### Key quotes
> “It might get hard to calculate pricing and usage if I stack multiple subscriptions.”

> “Yes definitely.”

### What changed after this interview
- Reinforced the need for a very simple onboarding flow
- Validated the usefulness of showing estimated savings clearly
- Confirmed that even solo developers think about subscription overlap
- Helped shape the messaging toward “future cost visibility” instead of only enterprise savings

## Interview 3 - Harshit Pathak
**Role:** SDE  
**Company:** Keploy  
**Date:** 2026-05-13

### Summary
Harshit currently uses ChatGPT Plus and Cursor Pro and previously experimented with Claude before canceling it. His monthly AI spend is around $35–40. He explained that many engineers independently purchase tools even when companies reimburse some subscriptions, mainly because they want flexibility and quicker access to features.

A major insight from this interview was that engineers knowingly keep overlapping subscriptions because each tool improves a different part of their workflow. However, Harshit also admitted that the overlap becomes difficult to justify financially over time.

He highlighted that the biggest pain point is tracking actual productivity impact versus passive usage. He also believed teams would genuinely share reports that clearly showed overlap, inactive subscriptions, or consolidation opportunities.

### Key quotes
> “Two tools overlap like 70–80% in functionality but you still keep both because each has one thing it does better.”

> “Nobody really has visibility into overlap or underutilized subscriptions.”

### What changed after this interview
- Strengthened the “AI stack efficiency” positioning
- Improved audit recommendations around consolidation opportunities
- Validated the idea of shareable audit reports for teams/founders
- Confirmed that AI subscription costs scale rapidly across engineering teams

## Overall patterns across interviews

Across all three interviews, a few patterns appeared consistently:

- Engineers rarely use just one AI tool anymore
- Different AI tools are preferred for different workflows
- Companies struggle to track real utilization across teams
- AI spending grows organically without centralized visibility
- Users care more about ROI and productivity than raw subscription cost
- Teams are open to tools that simplify AI spend management and consolidation

These interviews strongly validated the core premise behind AIAudit: AI tool adoption is growing quickly, but visibility into usage, overlap, and efficiency is still extremely limited.
